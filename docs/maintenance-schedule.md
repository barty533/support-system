# Scheduled Maintenance Guide

## Standard Maintenance Intervals

| Service | Interval |
|---------|---------|
| Engine oil & filter (conventional) | Every 5,000 miles or 6 months |
| Engine oil & filter (synthetic) | Every 7,500–10,000 miles or 12 months |
| Tyre rotation | Every 5,000–7,500 miles |
| Cabin air filter | Every 15,000–20,000 miles or annually |
| Engine air filter | Every 20,000–30,000 miles |
| Fuel filter (inline) | Every 30,000 miles (if serviceable) |
| Spark plugs (copper) | Every 30,000 miles |
| Spark plugs (iridium/platinum) | Every 60,000–100,000 miles |
| Brake fluid flush | Every 2 years or 30,000 miles |
| Coolant/antifreeze flush | Every 50,000 miles or 5 years |
| Transmission fluid (automatic) | Every 30,000–60,000 miles |
| Transmission fluid (manual) | Every 30,000–45,000 miles |
| Differential fluid | Every 30,000–60,000 miles |
| Transfer case fluid | Every 30,000 miles |
| Power steering fluid | Every 50,000 miles (hydraulic systems) |
| Brake pads (check) | Every 12,000 miles |
| Timing belt replacement | Every 60,000–100,000 miles (check manual) |
| Serpentine/drive belt | Every 60,000–100,000 miles or if cracked |

## Engine Oil

### Oil Viscosity Selection
- Follow the manufacturer's viscosity recommendation (found in owner's manual and oil filler cap).
- Common specifications: 0W-20, 5W-30, 5W-40, 10W-30.
- Using the wrong viscosity can affect fuel economy and engine protection.
- Do not mix conventional and synthetic oils — drain and refill with a single type.

### Oil Change Procedure
1. Warm the engine for 2–3 minutes to thin the oil.
2. Drain oil by removing the drain plug; allow full drainage (5–10 minutes).
3. Replace the drain plug with a new washer/gasket.
4. Remove the old oil filter; lubricate the new filter gasket with fresh oil before installing.
5. Refill with the correct grade and quantity; check the owner's manual for capacity.
6. Run the engine for 30 seconds, check for leaks, then recheck the oil level.

## Tyre Maintenance

### Tyre Rotation Patterns
- Front-wheel drive: cross rotation (front tyres move straight back; rear tyres cross to front).
- Rear-wheel drive: rearward cross (rear tyres move straight forward; front tyres cross to rear).
- AWD/4WD: full cross rotation recommended.
- Directional tyres: can only move front-to-rear on the same side.

### Tread Depth
- New tyres: 10/32" (approx. 8mm).
- Legal minimum in most regions: 2/32" (approx. 1.6mm).
- Recommended replacement threshold: 4/32" (approx. 3mm) for safety in wet conditions.
- Penny test: insert a penny with Lincoln's head facing down; if the top of his head is visible, replace the tyre.

### Tyre Pressure
- Check when tyres are cold (not driven in past 3 hours).
- Recommended pressure is on the driver's door jamb sticker, not on the tyre sidewall.
- Overinflation: increased centre tread wear, harsher ride, reduced grip.
- Underinflation: increased shoulder wear, overheating, increased rolling resistance, blowout risk.

## Brake System Maintenance

### Brake Pad Inspection
- Minimum pad thickness: 2mm. Replace at or before this point.
- Built-in wear indicators emit a high-pitched squeal when pads approach minimum thickness.
- Measure pad thickness through the caliper inspection window or wheel spoke if accessible.

### Brake Rotor Inspection
- Check for scoring, deep grooves, cracks, or heat spots (blue discolouration).
- Measure rotor thickness with a micrometer; discard if below the manufacturer's minimum specification.
- Lateral runout: maximum 0.05mm; excessive runout causes brake pedal pulsation.

### Brake Fluid
- Brake fluid is hygroscopic (absorbs moisture over time), which lowers its boiling point.
- Test moisture content with a brake fluid tester; replace if moisture content exceeds 3%.
- Never mix DOT ratings (e.g. DOT 3 and DOT 4 can be mixed; DOT 5 silicone-based is not compatible with DOT 3/4).
- Flush interval: every 2 years regardless of mileage.
